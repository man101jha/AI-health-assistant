MENTAL HEALTH — PATIENT GUIDE
===============================
Source: Adapted from MedlinePlus Health Topics (medlineplus.gov)
Topic Category: Mental and Behavioral Health
Last Reviewed: 2025

1. WHAT IS MENTAL HEALTH?
---------------------------
Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Good mental health does not mean being happy all the time. It means having the ability to cope with the challenges of everyday life, maintain fulfilling relationships, and function effectively.

2. COMMON MENTAL HEALTH CONDITIONS
-------------------------------------

Depression:
  - Persistent sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities
  - Affects 1 in 6 adults at some point in their lifetime
  - Highly treatable with therapy, medication, or a combination
  - Not a weakness or character flaw — it is a medical condition

Anxiety Disorders:
  - Includes generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety, and phobias
  - Characterized by excessive worry, fear, or panic that interferes with daily life
  - Most common category of mental health disorders
  - Very treatable with therapy (especially CBT) and medication

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD):
  - Develops after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event
  - Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, and avoidance behaviors
  - Can occur in anyone regardless of age, background, or resilience

Bipolar Disorder:
  - Causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and concentration
  - Episodes of mania (extreme highs) alternate with depressive episodes
  - Requires long-term treatment and medication management

Schizophrenia:
  - A serious disorder affecting how a person thinks, feels, and behaves
  - Symptoms include hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking
  - Requires psychiatric care and medication

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD):
  - Characterized by unwanted, recurring thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions)
  - Significantly interferes with daily functioning
  - Treatable with specific types of therapy and medication

3. WARNING SIGNS OF A MENTAL HEALTH PROBLEM
---------------------------------------------
Seek help if you or someone you know experiences:
- Eating or sleeping too much or too little
- Pulling away from people and activities you once enjoyed
- Having low or no energy
- Feeling numb or as if nothing matters
- Feeling hopeless or helpless
- Smoking, drinking, or using drugs more than usual
- Feeling unusually confused, forgetful, angry, upset, worried, or scared
- Yelling or fighting with family and friends
- Experiencing severe mood swings
- Hearing voices or believing things that are not true
- Thinking of harming yourself or others
- Inability to perform daily tasks

4. WHEN TO SEEK HELP IMMEDIATELY (CRISIS)
------------------------------------------
Call 911 or go to the nearest Emergency Department if:
- You or someone else is in immediate danger of harming themselves or others
- A person is unconscious or unresponsive

National Crisis Resources:
- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988 (24/7, free and confidential)
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline: 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)

5. TREATMENT OPTIONS
---------------------

Psychotherapy (Talk Therapy):
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps identify and change negative thought patterns
- Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): Effective for emotional regulation
- Exposure Therapy: Helps with phobias and PTSD
- Interpersonal Therapy (IPT): Focuses on relationships and social functioning
- Group Therapy: Peer support in a structured setting led by a therapist

Medication:
- Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs): Used for depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD
- Mood stabilizers (lithium, valproate): Used for bipolar disorder
- Antipsychotics: Used for schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and augmentation in depression
- Anti-anxiety medications: Buspirone, benzodiazepines (short-term use)
- Stimulants: Used for ADHD
Note: Medication should be managed by a licensed psychiatrist or physician. Do not stop psychiatric medications abruptly without consulting your provider.

Combined Treatment:
Most research shows that the combination of psychotherapy and medication is more effective than either treatment alone for many conditions.

6. SELF-HELP STRATEGIES FOR MENTAL WELLNESS
---------------------------------------------
- Exercise regularly: Physical activity reduces depression and anxiety symptoms
- Get adequate sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours per night for adults
- Eat a balanced diet: Nutrition affects mood and energy
- Practice mindfulness and meditation: Even 5-10 minutes daily helps
- Stay socially connected: Isolation worsens mental health
- Limit alcohol and avoid recreational drugs: Both worsen mental health conditions
- Set realistic goals and break tasks into manageable steps
- Practice gratitude: Write down three things you are grateful for each day

8. SUPPORTING SOMEONE WITH A MENTAL HEALTH CONDITION
------------------------------------------------------
- Listen without judgment
- Learn about their condition
- Encourage them to seek professional help
- Offer practical support (driving to appointments, helping with meals)
- Take care of your own mental health (caregiver burnout is real)
- Contact emergency services if you believe they are in immediate danger
- Never promise to keep suicidal thoughts a secret

9. REDUCING STIGMA
-------------------
Mental health conditions are medical conditions — not signs of weakness, poor character, or failure. Seeking help is a sign of strength. Many successful, high-functioning people live with and manage mental health conditions effectively with appropriate treatment.
