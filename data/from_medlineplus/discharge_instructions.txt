PATIENT DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS
================================
Document Type: Clinical Patient Education
Version: 2.0
Last Updated: January 2025

SECTION A — GENERAL POST-DISCHARGE GUIDELINES
===============================================

1. OVERVIEW
-----------
These discharge instructions are designed to guide you through your recovery at home following your hospital stay or outpatient procedure. Please read all instructions carefully. If you have questions, contact your care team before leaving the facility.

2. GENERAL RECOVERY GUIDELINES
--------------------------------
- Rest as much as possible during the first 24-48 hours after discharge
- Gradually increase your activity level as directed by your physician
- Do not drive for at least 24 hours after receiving anesthesia or sedation
- Do not make important legal or financial decisions within 24 hours of receiving anesthesia
- Have a responsible adult accompany you home and stay with you for the first 24 hours
- Follow all dietary restrictions as specified by your care team

3. WOUND CARE INSTRUCTIONS
---------------------------
Keeping your wound or incision site clean is essential to prevent infection.

Daily wound care:
  - Wash your hands thoroughly before and after touching the wound
  - Gently clean the wound with mild soap and water unless instructed otherwise
  - Pat dry with a clean cloth or sterile gauze
  - Apply fresh dressing as instructed by your nurse
  - Do not remove sutures, staples, or surgical tape (steri-strips) unless instructed

Things to avoid:
  - Do not submerge the wound in water (no baths, swimming, or hot tubs) until cleared by your provider
  - Avoid applying lotions, ointments, or creams not prescribed by your physician
  - Do not expose the wound to direct sunlight during healing
  - Avoid tight clothing over the wound area

4. SIGNS OF WOUND INFECTION (SEEK CARE IMMEDIATELY)
-----------------------------------------------------
Go to the Emergency Department or call 911 if you notice:
- Increasing redness, warmth, or swelling around the wound
- Wound edges separating or opening
- Pus or cloudy discharge from the wound
- Foul odor from the wound site
- Red streaks extending from the wound
- Fever above 101.5°F (38.6°C) with wound changes

5. PAIN MANAGEMENT
-------------------
Managing pain effectively is important for your recovery.

Prescribed pain medications:
  - Take medications exactly as prescribed — do not skip doses if in pain
  - Do not take more than the prescribed amount
  - If prescribed opioids, do not drive or operate machinery
  - Store opioid medications in a safe, secure location away from children
  - Dispose of unused opioids at a medication take-back program

Over-the-counter options:
  - Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin) or acetaminophen (Tylenol) may be used as directed
  - Do not take ibuprofen if you have kidney disease, stomach ulcers, or were advised against it
  - Do not exceed recommended daily dosages

Non-medication pain relief:
  - Apply ice packs to swollen areas (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off)
  - Elevate the affected area above heart level when possible
  - Use prescribed compression stockings as directed

6. ACTIVITY RESTRICTIONS
--------------------------
Activity restrictions vary depending on your procedure. Common restrictions include:

First week after most procedures:
  - No lifting objects heavier than 5 pounds (approximately the weight of a bag of flour)
  - No strenuous exercise, running, or contact sports
  - Short, gentle walks are encouraged to prevent blood clots
  - Rest frequently; fatigue is normal during recovery

Driving restrictions:
  - Do not drive while taking opioid pain medications
  - Do not drive for 24 hours after general anesthesia
  - Do not drive until you can perform an emergency stop comfortably

Return to work:
  - Desk or sedentary work: typically 1-2 weeks post-procedure (confirm with your doctor)
  - Physical or manual labor: typically 4-6 weeks (confirm with your doctor)

7. DIET AND NUTRITION
----------------------
- Begin with clear liquids if you feel nauseated after discharge
- Progress to soft foods (soups, mashed potatoes, yogurt) as tolerated
- Return to your regular diet once nausea subsides and appetite returns
- Stay well-hydrated — aim for 8 glasses of water per day unless fluid restricted
- Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours after anesthesia and while taking opioid medications

8. MEDICATIONS AFTER DISCHARGE
--------------------------------
- Take all prescribed medications as directed even if you feel better
- Complete the full course of any antibiotics prescribed
- Do not stop medications suddenly without consulting your provider
- Keep a current list of all your medications including OTC drugs and supplements
- Pick up prescriptions from the pharmacy before you run out

SECTION B — SPECIFIC POST-PROCEDURE INSTRUCTIONS
=================================================

POST-CARDIAC PROCEDURE INSTRUCTIONS
-------------------------------------
Following a cardiac catheterization, stent placement, or similar procedure:
- Keep the insertion site (groin or wrist) clean and dry for 48 hours
- Apply firm pressure if bleeding occurs at the site; call 911 if bleeding does not stop within 10 minutes
- Avoid lifting more than 10 pounds for 1 week
- Do not drive for 24 hours
- Take all heart medications as prescribed — do not stop blood thinners without consulting your cardiologist
- Watch for: chest pain, shortness of breath, irregular heartbeat, swelling in legs — go to ER immediately

POST-ORTHOPEDIC SURGERY INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------
Following joint replacement, fracture repair, or similar procedure:
- Use prescribed assistive devices (walker, crutches, cane) as instructed by physical therapy
- Attend all scheduled physical therapy appointments
- Keep surgical leg elevated when sitting or lying down to reduce swelling
- Apply ice as directed (not directly on skin — wrap in cloth)
- Do blood clot prevention exercises as taught (ankle pumps, leg lifts)
- Watch for signs of blood clot: calf pain, swelling, warmth in leg — go to ER immediately

9. FOLLOW-UP APPOINTMENTS
---------------------------
Your follow-up appointment has been scheduled. Please confirm the details provided by your care team. Missing your follow-up appointment can delay your recovery and increase the risk of complications.

If you do not have a scheduled follow-up appointment:
- Call our scheduling line: 1-800-HEALTH-1 to book within 7-14 days of discharge
- Specialist follow-up may be required in addition to primary care

10. WHEN TO CALL YOUR DOCTOR (Non-Emergency)
---------------------------------------------
Contact your care team at the phone number provided if you experience:
- Fever between 100°F and 101.4°F (37.8°C–38.5°C)
- Moderate increase in pain not controlled by prescribed medications
- Nausea or vomiting preventing you from taking medications
- Constipation for more than 3 days (common with opioid medications)
- Questions about your wound, medications, or activity restrictions

11. WHEN TO CALL 911 OR GO TO EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT
---------------------------------------------------
Seek emergency care IMMEDIATELY for:
- Severe chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Signs of stroke: sudden face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- High fever above 101.5°F (38.6°C)
- Severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, difficulty breathing, rash)
- Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
- Severe abdominal pain
- Signs of blood clot in leg or lungs
