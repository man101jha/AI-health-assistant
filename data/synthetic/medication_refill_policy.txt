MEDICATION REFILL POLICY
==========================
Document Type: Pharmacy and Clinical Policy
Version: 2.3
Last Updated: January 2025

1. OVERVIEW
-----------
This policy outlines the procedures for requesting, processing, and approving prescription medication refills. All refill requests are subject to clinical review and must comply with applicable state and federal regulations.

2. HOW TO REQUEST A MEDICATION REFILL
----------------------------------------
Patients may request refills through the following methods:

Method 1 - Patient Portal (Preferred):
  - Log in to portal.healthsystem.org
  - Navigate to "My Medications"
  - Select the medication and click "Request Refill"
  - Choose your preferred pharmacy
  - Allow 48-72 hours for processing

Method 2 - Pharmacy-Initiated Refill:
  - Ask your pharmacy to send a refill request electronically
  - Pharmacy contacts our office on your behalf
  - Processing time: 24-48 hours

Method 3 - Telehealth Visit:
  - Schedule a telehealth appointment
  - Provider reviews your medication and current health status
  - Prescription sent electronically to pharmacy upon approval

Method 4 - Phone Request:
  - Call our refill line: 1-800-HEALTH-2
  - Available 24/7 for refill requests
  - A nurse will review and process within one business day

3. MEDICATIONS ELIGIBLE FOR REFILL WITHOUT IN-PERSON VISIT
-------------------------------------------------------------
The following categories of medications may be refilled via telehealth or portal request for established patients:

Cardiovascular Medications:
  - Blood pressure medications (antihypertensives)
  - Cholesterol-lowering medications (statins)
  - Blood thinners (warfarin, for stable, monitored patients)

Endocrine Medications:
  - Thyroid medications (levothyroxine)
  - Oral diabetes medications (metformin, glipizide, sitagliptin)
  - Insulin refills (for established diabetic patients with recent A1C)

Respiratory Medications:
  - Maintenance inhalers (fluticasone, salmeterol, tiotropium)
  - Allergy medications

Mental Health Medications (Established Patients Only):
  - Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs)
  - Non-controlled anti-anxiety medications
  - Mood stabilizers (stable patients with recent labs)

Other:
  - Acid reflux medications (PPIs, H2 blockers)
  - Migraine preventive medications
  - Contraceptive medications

4. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCE REFILL POLICY
----------------------------------------
Controlled substances (Schedule II-V) have strict refill requirements:

Schedule II (e.g., Adderall, OxyContin, Ritalin):
  - CANNOT be refilled via phone, portal, or telehealth
  - Requires an in-person visit with the prescribing provider EVERY time
  - No early refills permitted (must wait until within 2 days of running out)
  - Original paper or electronic prescription required by law

Schedule III and IV (e.g., Xanax, Valium, Tramadol, Ambien):
  - In-person visit required at least every 3 months
  - Telehealth may be used for interim refills if in-person visit was within 90 days
  - Maximum of 5 refills per prescription
  - Early refills not permitted more than 2 days before the due date

Schedule V (e.g., cough medications with codeine):
  - Can be refilled via portal or telehealth for stable patients
  - Requires in-person visit annually

5. REFILL TIMING GUIDELINES
-----------------------------
- Do not wait until you are completely out of medication to request a refill
- Request refills when you have a 7-10 day supply remaining
- Most refills are processed within 24-72 business hours
- Holiday periods may cause processing delays of 1-2 additional days
- Early refill requests (more than 7 days early for non-controlled medications) may be denied by insurance

6. CONDITIONS THAT MAY PREVENT REFILL APPROVAL
------------------------------------------------
Your refill may be placed on hold or denied if:
- You have not had a required follow-up visit or lab work
- Your prescription has expired (most expire after 1 year)
- Your insurance requires prior authorization that has lapsed
- You have outstanding balances preventing service
- There are safety concerns based on your recent health information
- The medication requires updated lab results (e.g., kidney function for metformin)

7. MEDICATION RECONCILIATION
------------------------------
At every visit and refill review, providers will:
- Review all current medications including OTC drugs and supplements
- Check for potential drug interactions
- Assess whether current dosages remain appropriate
- Update your medication list in the electronic health record

8. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION (PA)
-----------------------------
Some medications require prior authorization from your insurance provider:
- PA requests are submitted by our office on your behalf
- Processing typically takes 3-5 business days
- Patients will be notified of approval or denial
- Appeals are available if authorization is denied
- Bridge supplies may be provided during the PA process when medically appropriate

9. REFILL DENIALS AND APPEALS
-------------------------------
If your refill request is denied:
- You will receive a notification explaining the reason
- You may appeal the decision by calling our clinical team
- If denied due to required visit, a telehealth appointment may be scheduled promptly
- For insurance denials, our staff will assist with the appeal process

10. MEDICATION DISPOSAL
------------------------
Do not flush unused medications down the toilet. Return unused or expired medications to:
- Our in-office medication disposal kiosk (available at all locations)
- Participating pharmacy take-back programs
- DEA-authorized collection sites
